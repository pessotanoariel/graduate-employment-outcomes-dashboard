# Methodology

## Overview

This project evaluates post-graduation outcomes of participants from a technology training program using a combination of graduate records and follow-up survey responses.

The public version of the repository uses synthetic datasets generated exclusively for demonstration purposes.

---

## Analytical Framework

The dashboard is organized around four analytical dimensions:

1. Employment Outcomes
2. IT Sector Insertion
3. Professional Development and Continuing Education
4. Participant Profile

The objective is to understand how graduates evolve after completing the training program and identify patterns associated with employment, technology careers, and continuing education.

---

## Data Sources

### Graduate Dataset

Contains demographic and program-related information:

* Graduate identifier
* Gender
* Age group
* Jurisdiction
* Course
* Cohort

### Survey Dataset

Contains follow-up survey responses:

* Current employment status
* Employment status before training
* IT sector employment
* Programming role
* Self-reported programming level
* Continuing education activities
* Perceived training impact

---

## Synthetic Data Generation

The original project used real operational and survey data.

For this public version:

* All original records were removed.
* Personally identifiable information was excluded.
* Synthetic datasets were generated from documented schemas.
* Probabilities and distributions were intentionally simplified.
* Results do not represent actual program performance.

The purpose of the synthetic datasets is to reproduce the analytical structure of the original project while preserving confidentiality.

---

## Key Metrics

### Employment Rate

Percentage of survey respondents reporting current employment.

### IT Employment Rate

Percentage of respondents working in technology-related positions.

### IT Conversion Rate

Percentage of respondents who transitioned from non-IT employment into IT-related roles after completing the program.

### Programming Employment

Percentage of respondents working in programming-related positions.

### Continuing Education Rate

Percentage of respondents who reported pursuing additional studies after graduation.

### Perceived Impact

Self-reported assessment of the training program's impact on professional development.

---

## Methodological Considerations

Survey-based evaluations are subject to response bias.

Additionally, different cohorts were surveyed at different points in time after graduation, which may affect comparability across groups.

Results should therefore be interpreted as descriptive indicators rather than causal estimates.

---

## Dashboard Design Principles

The dashboard follows a storytelling approach:

1. Employment outcomes after training.
2. Transition into the IT sector.
3. Continuing education and professional development.
4. Demographic profile of respondents.

This structure allows users to move from outcome indicators to contextual information in a logical sequence.
